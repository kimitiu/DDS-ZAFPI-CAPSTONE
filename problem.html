Problem

Manual reporting slows operational control.

The core business problem is not simply lack of automation. ZAFPI is managing production reporting across two sites with a fully manual, paper-based process. That creates reporting delay at T+1, weakens same-day visibility, and introduces a recurring need to reconcile information before it is trusted for review.

Paper-based processProduction information starts on paper, creating inconsistent timing and quality of record consolidation.
T+1 visibilityManagement sees key production information after delay instead of during the same operating day.
43% reconciliation burdenAround 43% of documents require weekly reconciliation, adding rework and reducing confidence in the baseline record.
57% utilization baselineThe current state suggests meaningful room for better review, tighter execution, and improved use of production capacity.
Why It Matters

In portfolio terms, this is the gap between collecting information and actually controlling the operation. The project target is to shorten that gap so that records are available in time for daily supervision, exception handling, and management action.
