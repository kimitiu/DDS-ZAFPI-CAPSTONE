Problem

Manual reporting slows operational control.

ZAFPI’s pre-project reporting model is fully manual, paper-based, and split across the farm site and the sorting and distribution center. That means production visibility arrives at T+1, operational review is delayed, and management has to reconcile records before trusting them for action.

Paper-first workflowProduction logs begin as paperwork rather than structured digital entries, making consolidation slower and less reliable.
T+1 reporting lagManagement cannot monitor the operation in near real time because reports land in the next reporting cycle.
43% reconciliation burdenAround 43% of documents require weekly reconciliation because mismatches and inconsistencies are common in the current state.
57% utilization baselineThe farm is operating at about 57% utilization, turning weak visibility into a larger business-performance issue.
Missing standard reportsThe as-is process lacks consistent monthly, quarterly, and annual summaries for structured management review.
Missing critical KPIsFeed consumption per house, mortality, and core performance indicators are not consistently tracked in a centralized system.

Business Case

The capstone frames the current-state problem as an operational-control issue, not just an IT inconvenience. Underutilization, delayed reporting, and weak data discipline combine into slower decisions, more rework, and a large estimated annual lost-revenue story of about ₱105.5 million, excluding spoilage and breakage.
