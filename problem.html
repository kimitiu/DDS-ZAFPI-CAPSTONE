Problem

Manual reporting slows visibility, review, and control.

Before Project Farmbrite, ZAFPI relied on a fully manual, paper-based production reporting process split across the farm site and the sorting and distribution center. That setup delayed visibility, created weekly reconciliation work, and limited house-level monitoring for key measures like feed and mortality.

The problem is not only administrative. It affects how quickly management can understand what is happening on the farm, how reliably records can be consolidated, and how confidently the business can respond to emerging issues.

Core issue

The current-state model produces T+1 visibility, inconsistent house-level tracking, and no formal consolidated monthly, quarterly, or yearly reporting baseline for management control.

Current-state pain points

Paper-based source capture

Production data begins as paperwork, which means consolidation and review depend on later encoding rather than immediate visibility.

Weak feed visibility per house

Feed consumption is not tracked consistently at the house level, weakening the ability to spot mismatches and operational issues early.

No consolidated reporting rhythm

The current setup lacks formal consolidated monthly, quarterly, or yearly reporting, making trend review and management control harder.

04

Poor visibility

Management cannot respond with the confidence or speed needed for proactive daily operational control.

What the current state creates

The reporting model turns routine production data into a lagging management view.

  • Late review of production output and issues
  • Manual effort before data can be trusted
  • Weak standardization across sites and houses
  • Limited accountability for timely encoding and validation

What the project needs to correct

The capstone must solve process discipline as much as visibility.

  • Same-day operational reporting instead of T+1 lag
  • Less than 15% reconciliation after stabilization
  • 95%+ daily encoding compliance target
  • Clearer feed, mortality, and house-level review signals

Problem statement
ZAFPI’s reporting problem is a business visibility problem. Manual paperwork, T+1 reporting, weak per-house controls, and heavy reconciliation work combine into slower decisions and weaker operational confidence.
