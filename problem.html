Problem

Manual reporting slows operational control.

ZAFPI’s pre-project reporting model is fully manual, paper-based, and split across the farm site and the sorting and distribution center. That means production visibility arrives at T+1, operational review is delayed, and management has to spend time reconciling records before they are trusted.

Paper-first workflowProduction logs begin as paperwork rather than structured digital entries, making consolidation slower and less reliable.
T+1 reporting lagManagement cannot monitor the operation in near real time because reports land on the next reporting cycle.
43% reconciliation burdenAround 43% of documents require weekly reconciliation because mismatches and inconsistencies are common in the current state.
57% utilization baselineThe farm is operating at about 57% utilization, which means weak visibility is directly tied to a larger performance opportunity.
Missing standard reportsThe as-is process lacks consistent monthly, quarterly, and annual reporting, limiting structured review and trend analysis.
Missing critical KPIsFeed consumption per house, mortality, and performance KPIs are not consistently tracked in a centralized way.
Why It Matters

The capstone frames the current-state problem as an operational control issue, not just an IT inconvenience. Underutilization, delayed reporting, and weak data discipline combine into slower decisions, more rework, and a large estimated annual lost-revenue story of about ₱105.5 million excluding spoilage and breakage.

Target Shift
Reporting speedT+1 moves to same-day reporting.
Visibility targetOperational visibility should be available within 1 hour after cut-off.
Reconciliation targetWeekly reconciliation should fall from 43% to below 15%.
Data completenessStructured daily reporting should reach at least 98% completeness.
Utilization targetThe project story targets improvement from 57% toward roughly 75% utilization.
