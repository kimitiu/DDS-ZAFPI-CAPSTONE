Farmbrite Demo
System debrief

A same-day management system built around reporting discipline.

Project Farmbrite combines business process redesign with a practical system approach: structured digital production capture, validations, audit trails, dashboards, and reporting that support operational review within the same day.

The goal is not only to digitize forms. It is to create a control model where source capture, checks, accountability, and management visibility all reinforce each other.

System direction

Use Farmbrite as the enabling platform for digital production capture, same-day visibility, reporting structure, and stronger data integrity across the ZAFPI operation.

Scope 24 houses
The system concept covers the full production footprint rather than a partial reporting subset.
Visibility goal Same-day
Operational review should no longer wait for T+1 consolidation cycles.
Compliance target 95%+
Daily encoding discipline is part of the operating model, not an optional behavior.
Reconciliation target <15%
Lower reconciliation is one of the clearest measures of improved control and cleaner data flows.
System concept

Digital production capture

Daily production, feed, and mortality data move into structured digital forms instead of staying trapped in paper-first workflows.

Validation and audit trails

Checks, correction handling, and traceable activity improve trust in the records and make review more consistent.

Dashboards and reporting

Management gets same-day visibility through dashboards and reports that support routine operational review.

Operational review loop

The system closes the gap between source capture and action by giving managers earlier, cleaner signals to review.

Data integrity discipline

Standardized definitions, validations, and reporting rules improve consistency across houses and operating locations.

Business value

Stronger visibility supports better planning, cleaner controls, and a clearer path toward higher utilization after stabilization.

As-is process

The original process relies on manual handling before information becomes useful to management.

  • Source data starts on paper
  • Encoding happens later in the cycle
  • Consolidation is delayed and fragile
  • Management visibility lands after the fact

To-be process

The redesigned process creates a tighter same-day management loop.

  • Source capture happens in a structured digital flow
  • Same-day input supports earlier visibility
  • Automated consolidation improves consistency
  • Review, accountability, and reporting become part of the daily rhythm
